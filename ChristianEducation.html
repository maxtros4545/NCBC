Master of Divinity in Biblical Studies

About this Program

The Master in Christian Education prepares educators and ministry leaders to integrate biblical principles with teaching strategies. This program equips students with theological knowledge, curriculum development skills, and leadership abilities to nurture faith-based learning environments. Graduates will be prepared to teach, lead, and disciple in Christian schools, churches, and ministries.

Program Highlights

Course Description

This program provides advanced training in Christian pedagogy, educational leadership, and spiritual formation. Students will study biblical foundations, instructional methods, and curriculum design tailored for faith-based education. Through research, practical application, and mentorship, graduates will develop the skills necessary to create transformative learning experiences in Christian academic and ministry settings.

    Core Courses

    • Biblical Foundations of Christian Education – Theological perspectives on teaching and learning.
    • Christian Curriculum Development – Designing faith-based educational materials.
    • Leadership in Christian Education – Managing and directing Christian institutions.
    • Teaching Methods in Christian Schools – Effective instructional strategies for educators.
    • Spiritual Formation in Education – Integrating faith into the learning process.

Learning outcomes

The Master in Christian Education program equips graduates with a strong biblical and theological foundation for teaching and leadership in faith-based settings. Students develop effective instructional strategies, curriculum design skills, and administrative abilities for Christian schools and ministries. They also gain expertise in spiritual mentorship, discipleship, and community outreach, preparing them to impact both local and global educational missions. Through this program, graduates learn to integrate faith into education, fostering holistic spiritual growth in learners. With a focus on leadership, pastoral care, and ethical teaching, this program prepares educators to serve effectively in churches, schools, and Christian organizations.

    Learning Outcomes

    • Apply biblical principles to teaching and curriculum development.
    • Design and implement faith-based educational programs.
    • Lead and manage Christian schools, ministries, and training programs.
    • Integrate theology with modern educational psychology.
    • Equip and mentor students for spiritual growth and discipleship.

Vocation and Career Opportunities

Graduates of the Master in Christian Education program can pursue careers in faith-based teaching, leadership, and ministry. Opportunities include roles as Christian school teachers, curriculum developers, school administrators, discipleship coordinators, and ministry leaders. They can also serve as missionary educators, church education directors, and trainers in Christian organizations.

    Career Paths in Christian Education

    • Christian School Teacher – Educating students with a faith-based curriculum.
    • Curriculum Developer – Designing biblical and theological educational materials.
    • School Administrator – Leading and managing Christian schools and training programs.
    • Discipleship Coordinator – Developing programs to mentor and nurture faith formation.
    • Missionary Educator – Teaching and training in global Christian education initiatives.

Program Highlights

The Master in Christian Education program provides a strong biblical foundation, equipping students with effective teaching strategies, leadership skills, and curriculum development expertise. Emphasizing spiritual growth, discipleship, and ministry leadership, the program prepares graduates for impactful roles in Christian schools, churches, and global educational missions, fostering faith-based learning environments.

    Core Advantages of the Program

    • Strong Biblical and Theological Foundation – Equipping educators with scriptural knowledge.
    • Practical Training – Hands-on experience in curriculum design and teaching.
    • Leadership Development – Preparing leaders for Christian schools and ministries.
    • Flexible Learning – Designed for working professionals with adaptable study options.
    • Research and Application – Opportunities to explore and implement Christian education principles.
