Young Couples Ministry

Ministry Among Young Couples

Our ministry is dedicated to supporting and nurturing young couples in their journey of faith, love, and family. We provide a space where couples can grow spiritually, build strong relationships, and find a supportive community.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower young couples with guidance, resources, and support to build strong, loving, and lasting relationships. We strive to foster communication, trust, and growth through education, shared experiences, and a supportive community, helping couples navigate challenges and create a fulfilling future together.

Our Vision

Our vision for the Young Couples Ministry is to create a space for young couples and young families to be rooted in Christ and planted in community. We believe that Christ-centered relationships are the cornerstone of a thriving family and a flourishing life.

About Us

We are a community of couples who are at various stages of their journey together. Whether you’re recently married, have young children, or are planning to start a family, you’ll find a welcoming space where you can connect with others who share similar experiences and values.

What to Expect

  • Couples' Workshops: Strengthen your relationship, improve communication, and deepen your connection.
  • Family-Focused Events: Bond with your children and create lasting memories together.
  • Bible Studies: Discover biblical wisdom for your relationship.
  • Community Support: Connect with other young couples and families for encouragement and friendship.
  • Date Nights: Enjoy special date nights designed to rekindle the romance.

Discipleship: Three Keys to Encourage Young Couples

As believers, we are called into a process of ongoing change and transformation. We are called to be disciples of Jesus – to learn from him and continually become more and more like him. Discipleship is a maturing process of moving from milk to meat. The encouraging reality is we are not on this journey alone. The Spirit enables us to walk with fellow believers.

Focus on Relationship

Let the primary focus be on cultivating a strong relationship with the younger couple. A vibrant relationship takes time, attention, and care...

Create a Safe Environment

The next important area to consider is creating and maintaining a safe environment for the younger couple to share...

  • Don’t assume you understand them. Periodically ask if they feel understood by you.
  • Don’t problem solve too quickly, if at all – “be swift to hear, slow to speak” (James 1:19).
  • Be authentic about not having it all figured out – continue to grow and learn.
  • Seek to limit ongoing questions that can feel probing – instead share open-ended statements or questions.
  • Seek to understand rather than condemn. Judgment and defensiveness block vulnerability.
  • Allow the Word and Holy Spirit to bring conviction.
  • Be patient – growth and change take time.
  • Seek to empathize and affirm.
  • Meet individually (man with man, woman with woman) on occasion to allow different experiences and sharing.

Embracing the Discipline of Jesus

Part of growing as disciples of Jesus is embracing the discipline of Jesus. This is true whether it comes to spending time in prayer or time discipling younger couples. Jesus willingly took upon himself the constraints of living in a human body, including the need for food, sleep, and solitude.

Jesus engaged both times of teaching his disciples (Matthew 16:5-28, Mark 9:30-50) and individual prayer (Mark 1:35; Luke 5:15-16).

It is important to be clear about what is and what is not your responsibility when discipling younger couples. Lack of clarity can lead to:

  • Burnout
  • Stress
  • Dependency
  • Unrealistic role expectations

Remember, you are not responsible for change in a young couple’s marriage—God is. Your responsibility is to:

  • Be present, engaged, and responsive
  • Spend consistent, quality, personal time with Jesus
  • Be open about what is outside your role

There may be times when professional counseling is needed, such as in cases of:

  • Ongoing addictions
  • Indications of abuse
  • Past or present trauma
  • Persistent disengagement
  • Unhealthy emotional control

Encouraging a younger couple to seek professional help can be a difficult but necessary conversation. Supporting them in understanding the value of professional counseling is part of providing sound guidance.
