Master of Divinity in Biblical Studies

About this Program

The Master of Divinity in Biblical Studies is designed for individuals seeking a deep understanding of Scripture, theology, and pastoral leadership. This program equips students with the knowledge and skills needed for ministry, biblical teaching, and scholarly research. Through a comprehensive curriculum, students will engage in theological reflection, develop effective communication and leadership abilities, and apply biblical principles to real-world contexts. With opportunities for internships, mentorship, and global outreach, graduates will be well-prepared for pastoral roles, academic careers, and various forms of Christian ministry

Program Highlights

Course Description

This program offers advanced theological education, focusing on biblical interpretation, pastoral care, and ministry leadership. Students engage in rigorous biblical study, practical ministry training, and ethical decision-making. It prepares graduates for roles in church leadership, academia, and missions while deepening their understanding of the Christian faith.

    Core Courses

    • Advanced Biblical Hermeneutics
    • Old and New Testament Theology
    • Pastoral Ministry and Leadership
    • Church History and Doctrine
    • Homiletics and Public Speaking

Learning outcomes

Graduates will develop strong leadership skills, deep biblical understanding, and effective communication abilities. They will be equipped to counsel, mentor, and lead congregations, implement strategic ministry initiatives, and foster spiritual growth. This program prepares students to navigate modern ministry challenges with wisdom, compassion, and resilience.

    Key Learning Objectives

    • Analyze and interpret biblical texts using scholarly methods
    • Develop effective leadership and pastoral care skills
    • Communicate biblical truths through preaching and teaching
    • Apply Christian ethics in personal and professional life
    • Engage in theological research for ministry and academia

Vocation and Career Opportunities

This degree opens doors to leadership roles in churches, seminaries, and Christian organizations. Graduates may serve as pastors, theologians, missionaries, or educators. It also prepares individuals for advanced theological studies or counseling roles within faith-based institutions.

    Career Paths

    • Senior Pastor or Associate Pastor
    • Bible Teacher or Seminary Professor
    • Missionary or Evangelist
    • Christian Counselor
    • Theological Researcher
